Constraints

  • The first syllable is always the stressed one (obviously, if ur singing, do whatever u want)
  • Syllables must follow the structure (C)V(n, m, l). The first consonant can only be omitted in first syllable. A word cannot start with /ji/
  • The combinations /ij/ & /nm/ are not allowed

Spelling

When a letter is repeated due to derivation/composition/whatever, they get merged into one

Capitallization

  • First letter in sentense, first letter of proper or foreign words

Numbers

Numbers (which are base 6), are written in arabic form. Do whatever you want, but "," won't be part of a number

Punctuation

⟨Untitled Conlang Experiment⟩ uses the same punctuation as most european languages

Syntax

⟨Untitled Conlang Experiment⟩ has an SVO (subject, verb, object) structure. Adjectives follow nouns, adverbs & predicatives follow verbs. There's no distinction between adjectives, nouns & pronouns: the first one is treated as a noun, and the rest as modifiers. The one exception are numerals, which precede their noun

Proper Names & Foreign Words

Both proper names & foreign words must be used as a modifier/adjective after a noun, and are always capitalized
